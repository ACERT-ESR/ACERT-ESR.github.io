PulseTune¶

class Experiments.PulseTune(**kwargs)¶

The Pulse Tune experiment measures T1. There are two pulses in each sequence, separated by a time t1, known as the mixing time. The first is a pi/2 pulse, the second pi. Over the course of the experiment, we change the distance between the pulses. This version does not use any phase cycling to tune the results.

averages¶

The number of shots to be averaged for each step in the phase-cycle.

phasecycle¶

A list of phase combinations denoting the desired phasecycle. The phase cycle is just (0,0).

pulse_length¶
This is the length of the pi/2 pulse. The pi pulse
will be twice this length.

The nominal duration [s] of the pulse.

rep_rate¶

The shot repetition rate [\(s^{-1}\)]

T¶

The time increment [s] used to step out the delay \(t_1\) between the center of the pulses. This increment should be positive.

t1_min¶

The minimum delay [s] between the center of the pulses.

t1_steps¶

The number of steps of the inter-pulse delay \(t_1\).

After acquisition, the following additional properties are defined.

data¶

The acquired data, as a complex-valued \(n\times m\times l\) array, where \(n\) is the number of steps in the phase cycle, \(m\) is the number of \(t_1\) steps, and \(l\) is the record length.

ended¶

A textual representation of the time at which the experiment finished.

field¶

The nominal magnetic field [T] at which the experiment was performed.
