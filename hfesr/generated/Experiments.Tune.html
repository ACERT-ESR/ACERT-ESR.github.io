Tune¶

class Experiments.Tune(**kwargs)¶

This resonator tuning aid generates a sequence of four pulses at 50, 100, 150, and 200 ns after the acquisition trigger with phases of 0, 90, 180, and 270 degrees, respectively, and renders a live display of the received quadrature signal on the screen. In cw mode, the transceiver will not generate pulses but still switch the phase of the transmitted millimeter-wave signal.

This class is intended for tuning the millimeter-wave resonator by monitoring the crosstalk picked up by the receiver and/or the reflected power in the high-power arm (using an external oscilloscope).

The number of shots to be averaged and/or the shot repetition rate may be specified by changing the averages and reprate members. The default is single shot acquisition at a repetition rate of 1000/s. The tuning function is terminated by hitting the return key.

reprate¶

The shot repetition rate [\(s^{-1}\)]

averages¶

The number of shots to be averaged for each step in the phase-cycle.

For example, from the hfesr command-line, one could run this with:

tune.reprate=5000
tune.averages=50
tune.run()
